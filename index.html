Improving Quality of Service Using Caching Joints Shortcut Routing 

This research has investigated how to reduce latency in Information-Centric Networking (ICN is an emerging future network architecture that has the potential to solve increasingly acute issues caused by the exponential increase of network traffic). I devised two algorithms by improving the routing and placement of data packets. This work could ameliorate the quality of service, especially as it related to streaming services. According to the result of extensive experiments and simulations, the proposed algorithms noticeably outperformed five existing algorithms concerning latency, cache hits, and total processed data packets.

Improve Efficiency of Data Collection in Smart Cities by Optimizing the Deployment of Data Centers

This project involved simulating the process of data collection (obtain the filling level of garbage bins’ capacity and humidity of vegetation along the roads) in smart cities. In such a city, taxis are used to carry data from infrastructures to data centers. Based on an analysis of the real-life trajectory of taxis, I simulated two vehicular ad-hoc networks. Moreover, I proposed three schemes that could improve the deployment of data centers to increase the efficiency of data collection. This research proved the feasibility of utilizing taxis to collect useful data and has the potential to facilitate the maintenance and update of infrastructure.
