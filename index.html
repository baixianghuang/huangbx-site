Publications

Baixiang Huang, Bryan Hooi. Traffic Accident Prediction using Graph Neural Networks: New Datasets and the TRAVEL Model, NUS Master’s Thesis, 2021. [Data]

In this project, I construct a new set of four graph-based traffic accident datasets, along with two benchmark tasks (accident occurrence prediction and accident severity prediction). I then comprehensively evaluate a variety of state-of-the-art GNN variants using the created datasets. 

Furthermore, we propose our novel Traffic Accident Vulnerability Estimation via Linkage (TRAVEL) model, which is designed to capture angular and directional information from road networks. We demonstrate that our proposed model consistently outperforms the baselines by a large margin. 

Baixiang Huang, Wei Liu, Tian Wang, et al. Deployment Optimization of Data Centers in Vehicular Networks[J]. IEEE Access, 2019. [IEEE Xplore]

This paper involved simulating the data collection process (e.g., obtaining the filling level of garbage bins’ capacity and humidity of vegetation along the roads) in smart cities. In such a city, taxis are used to carry data from infrastructures to data centers. 

Based on an analysis of the real-world trajectories of taxis, I simulated two vehicular ad-hoc networks. Moreover, we proposed three schemes that could improve the deployment of data centers to increase the efficiency of data collection. This research proved the feasibility of utilizing taxis to collect useful data and could facilitate the maintenance and update of infrastructure.

Baixiang Huang, Anfeng Liu, Chengyuan Zhang, et al. Caching Joint Shortcut Routing to Improve Quality of Service for Information-Centric Networking[J]. Sensors, 2018. [link]

This research has investigated how to reduce latency in Information-Centric Networking (ICN). This emerging future network architecture has the potential to solve increasingly acute issues caused by the exponential increase of network traffic. We devised two algorithms by improving the routing and placement of data packets. 

This work could improve the quality of service, especially as it relates to streaming services. The experiments and simulations show that the proposed algorithms noticeably outperformed five existing algorithms concerning latency, cache hits, and total processed data packets.
